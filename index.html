About BMI:

The Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on an individual's weight and height.

While it can provide a general indication of whether a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese, it has limitations and doesn't account for factors like muscle mass, bone density, and distribution of fat.

Therefore, while BMI can be a useful screening tool, it should not be the sole determinant of a person's health. Consulting with a healthcare professional is recommended for a more comprehensive assessment of one's health and fitness.

BMI Categories:

Underweight: BMI less than 18.5

Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9

Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9

Obese: BMI of 30 or greater
